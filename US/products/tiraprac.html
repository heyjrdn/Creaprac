Tiraprac

Curved bullnose for floor and wall tiles

Finish the edge of tiles that meet on the corner of two walls

Ceramic tile does not have glaze on its edge, only on its surface.

TIRAPRAC is a profile that is use to finish the edge of tiles being installed over the 90 degree corner of two meeting walls, hiding the white or red color clay of the unglazed edge.

On the past, this was achieved by making a 45 degree cut to the edges of the meeting tiles, but with the disadvantage of a very fragile finish, very susceptible to get broken.

TIRAPRAC is composed of a curved part, that covers the edge of the joining tiles and a flat part with holes, that remains under one of the joining tiles, the adhesive and the wall.

The adhesive passes through the holes, solidifying into a form of cement nails, achieving a fixed and solid installation.

TIRAPRAC is manufactured in rigid PVC in 18 solid colors, 17 marble colors, 3 wood colors and 6 aluminum finishes, giving you a lot of combination or contrast options to choose from.

It has a length of 8" and is offered in 2 heights, 3⁄8" for most world wide tiles and 1⁄4" for some South American and Asian tiles.
