Juntaprac

Expansion joint for floor tiles

Avoids cracks due to settling of the construction

Any construction has natural movements due to settling and dilatation.

That is why expansion joints are left on concrete floors, to distibute on them those expansion movements.

JUNTAPRAC is a profile manufactured with a double extrusion process, where 2 rigid pvc profiles in L form and a flexible pvc profile are fused together.

When a JUNTAPRAC is installed instead of a grout line, that passes close or over an expansion joint of the concrete floor where the tile covering is being installed, the natural movements of the construction are absorbed on the JUNTAPRAC, avoiding future cracks on the tile covering.

JUNTAPRAC should not be installed in each of the grout lines, but only on the grout lines outlined in the past paragraph.

The flexible profile of the JUNTAPRAC remains visible, and the flat rigid pvc profiles with holes remain unseen between the concrete floor, the adhesive and the tile covering.

This product comes in a height of 0.39", so it is recommended for all tile coverings with a thickness between 0.35" and 0.43". It is available in 6 colors with a length of 8".
